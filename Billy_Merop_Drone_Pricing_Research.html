02

The Pricing Problem

You're navigating several interconnected challenges that make pricing difficult. Here's how they interact:

Market Ignorance Exploitation

Competitors are leveraging client ignorance to inflate margins well above fair market rates. Without a clear understanding of true cost structures, your clients can't distinguish between fair pricing and exploitative pricing. This creates an opening: if you can show clients the real math, you become the trusted option.

Equipment Cost Dualism (US vs. Chinese)

The US vs. Chinese drone legislation creates a two-tier equipment market. Chinese drones (DJI) are cheaper but increasingly restricted for federal-adjacent work. US-made alternatives carry a significant price premium. This directly affects your capital requirements and hourly cost allocation.

Federal Funding Trigger (NDAA)

NDAA rules require US-made drones for any work touching federal money. This creates both a barrier for competitors still on Chinese equipment AND a competitive moat for you if your fleet is compliant. More on this in Section 5.

Enterprise Contract Accuracy

At the enterprise scale you're operating at ($200K-$300K contracts), a 10% pricing error means $25K left on the table or $25K in lost margin. Accurate cost accounting isn't optional at this level -- it's the difference between a good year and a bad one.

Revenue Predictability Gap

Without stable recurring revenue, growth planning becomes guesswork. Retainer models create both cash flow predictability and deeper client relationships. Section 6 digs into how to build this out.

The Opportunity

You need a pricing framework that accurately reflects your true costs, accounts for regulatory complexity, positions you to capture premium pricing for NDAA-compliant work, and builds stable recurring revenue underneath everything else.

D. Software & Post-Processing

Post-Processing Cost Structure

Post-processing adds 15 - 40% to overall job costs. A 2-hour flight can generate 8+ hours of processing work.

E. Regulatory & Compliance

Compliance & Administrative Burden
  • FAA Part 107: Initial cert + renewal every 24 months
  • Part 107 Waivers: No fee, but 90+ day processing (opportunity cost)
  • Airspace Auth: Required for Class B, C, D, E controlled airspace
  • NDAA Compliance: Documentation required for federal-adjacent work

The hidden cost: time spent on waivers, compliance paperwork, regulatory research. Many operators underestimate this. Your CHST certification gives you an edge here -- you already understand construction site safety requirements better than most drone operators.

The Full Picture

Your all-in hourly cost: equipment depreciation ($10-40/hr) + insurance ($2-5/hr) + labor ($20-30/hr as solo) + overhead ($50-70/hr) + post-processing (15-40% of job cost) + travel + compliance admin. Any hourly rate that doesn't account for all of these is leaving money on the table or losing money outright.

Pricing Models

Per-Hour, Per-Acre, Per-Deliverable

Per-Hour: Most common for photography, videography, and time-sensitive work. Easy to quote but clients sometimes balk at high rates without understanding the cost structure.

Per-Acre: Natural fit for your mapping and surveying work. Predictable for both sides. Just make sure small projects have minimum fees to stay profitable.

Per-Deliverable: Growing model for data-intensive work. Charges for outcomes (3D models, orthomosaics, reports) rather than time. This is where the highest margins live -- but requires clear scope definition.

Your Position

Your primary work (surveying/mapping) fits per-acre pricing naturally. Your thermal cert opens hourly work. As you build recurring relationships, retainer pricing (Section 6) becomes the third leg of the stool.

Legislation Landscape

The regulatory environment fundamentally changed on December 22, 2025. Understanding this is critical to your pricing strategy and competitive positioning.

The NDAA Timeline

What Happened and Why It Matters
  • FY2025 NDAA: Required a security audit of DJI within 1 year
  • Dec 22, 2025: Audit never completed. DJI added to FCC Covered List
  • FCC Went Broader: ALL foreign-manufactured drones and critical components added -- not just DJI
  • Retroactive Power: FCC can revoke previously certified devices
  • Loopholes Closed: "Clone" rebranding strategies explicitly prohibited

Current Equipment Status

What's Allowed vs. Restricted

Existing DJI Drones

  • Still physically flyable under Part 107 (no FAA grounding)
  • CANNOT be used on federally funded projects
  • No new imports or sales

US-Made / NDAA-Compliant

  • Can be used on ANY project, including federal
  • Premium pricing justified by market access
  • Higher capital cost, but unlocks revenue your competitors can't touch

The Federal Funding Trigger

What Counts as "Federal Funding"

ANY federal money in the pipeline triggers NDAA compliance:

  • Federal grants
  • Highway funding
  • FEMA funding
  • Infrastructure grants (IIJA, etc.)
  • Any government-adjacent project receiving federal dollars

When triggered: must use US-made drones, ground control, and comms equipment. No exceptions. Not a gray area.

Your Competitive Advantage
  • Competitors using DJI are locked out of federal/grant-funded work -- a growing market segment
  • A US-made fleet justifies premium pricing on government and grant-funded projects
  • Your CHST + thermography certs position you for construction and infrastructure (heavy federal funding flows)
  • This regulatory advantage compounds over time as infrastructure spending increases
  • The equipment investment pays for itself through market access your competitors can't match

The Retainer Model

Typical Structure

  • Monthly Retainer: $1,500 - $5,000 per active site
  • Frequency: Weekly or biweekly flights
  • Portfolio Capacity: Solo operator can manage 5-10 active contracts

The Math

5 sites x $2,500/month x 12 = $150,000/year

10 sites x $2,500/month x 12 = $300,000/year

Per-Visit Alternative

$500 - $2,000 per visit. Retainer clients get the lower end (~$500-750/visit) vs. $1,500-2,000 for one-off flights. The discount is worth it for the predictability.

Target clients: Construction companies, property managers, utility companies, municipal governments.

Growth Path

Minimum monthly burn for operations: ~$19,425 (with small team) or significantly less as solo.
